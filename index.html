Referral Network
Your referral network is your marketing department in year 1. Build it before you open your doors.
The Bodija opportunity: The teaching hospital is simultaneously your biggest competitor and your best referral source. Their physiotherapy department has 1–4 week waits. Post-discharge patients need ongoing private care. Build relationships inside that hospital in week 1 — before you open.
Referral source value map
H
Teaching Hospital — internal contacts Priority 1
Identify 3–5 registrars and senior physiotherapists at LAUTECH. These individuals see overflow cases they cannot accommodate. Offer to take their private referrals with a formal referral letter system. A quarterly thank-you gift (₦5–10k at Eid/Christmas) maintains the relationship.
Target: 2–3 referrals/month by Month 3 · Post-surgical packages = ₦52–96k each
Rx
Pharmacies (5 within 1km) Priority 1
Pharmacists are the trusted first-contact for musculoskeletal pain in Nigeria. Visit personally in week 2 pre-launch. Leave branded referral cards. Offer the first referred patient a free initial assessment — this closes the loop and earns lasting trust.
Target: 5 pharmacy partners by opening day · 1–2 referrals per pharmacy per month
Dr
Orthopedic Surgeons & GPs Priority 2
Private orthopedic surgeons in Ibadan operate on patients who need structured physiotherapy rehab. Post-surgical patients are motivated, compliant, and use packages. Visit 2–3 surgeons personally. Bring a one-page clinic profile and referral pads.
Target: 2 surgeon partnerships by Month 2 · Post-surgical = 6–12 session packages minimum
Fx
Gyms & Fitness Centers Priority 2

Competitive Landscape
Four players in the Bodija field. Here is how to think about each — and where Goshen wins decisively.
Positioning map — personalization vs. access speed
Competitor price comparison
Wait time comparison
UCH — University College Hospital Closer · Highest threat
Profile: Nigeria's most prominent teaching hospital. Closest to Goshen. High brand trust, institutional referrals, low cost (₦2–5k), high legitimacy.
Fatal weakness: 1–4 week wait times, severely overloaded, generic protocols, no personalisation, no follow-up. Post-discharge patients need ongoing private care they cannot provide.
Goshen's play: Be their private overflow. Build relationships with 3–5 registrars and senior physios personally — before opening. Never compete on price. Position as "the structured rehab option after UCH."
Risk: If UCH adds a private wing, reassess immediately.
LAUTECH Teaching Hospital Further · Secondary target
Profile: Further from the clinic than UCH but still draws patients from Bodija. Similar price point (₦2–5k), similar wait times, similar weaknesses.
Goshen's play: Lower referral priority than UCH but worth 1–2 personal contacts in the physio department. Their post-discharge patients in the Bodija area are Goshen's natural target.
Opportunity: LAUTECH staff are often more receptive to private partnerships than UCH due to smaller bureaucracy.
Established private clinic (Bodija) Medium threat
Likely profile: ₦6–10k/session. 2+ years operational. Has some patient base and hospital relationships.
Likely weakness: No digital presence, no outcome tracking, no WhatsApp booking, variable quality.
Goshen's play: Outrank them on Google within 6 months. Win on professionalism and access speed. If they have unhappy former staff, those are potential hires.
Two smaller private clinics Lower threat
Likely profile: Solo physiotherapist, ₦4–8k/session, limited equipment, informal setup.
Their patients: Price-sensitive segments — not Goshen's primary target.
Goshen's play: They validate demand. When they are overwhelmed or close, their patients will seek quality — be the obvious upgrade. One may be open to a formal referral agreement.

Local grants & funding

T
Tony Elumelu Foundation Grant
$5,000 USD (non-refundable) + mentorship
Annual application (typically opens Q1). Requires a business plan, 6 months operational history, and social impact narrative. Goshen's recovery time reduction data fits perfectly. Apply at Month 10–12.
B
Bank of Industry (BOI) — Healthcare SME Loan
₦2M–₦50M at 5–9% interest (concessionary)
BOI healthcare fund for medical SMEs. Not a grant but highly subsidized. Requires CAC registration, 6 months of bank statements, business plan. Target Month 8–10 for equipment upgrade or third physio hire.
N
NIRSAL Microfinance Bank — Healthcare Loan
₦500k–₦3M at subsidized rates
Government-backed fund for healthcare businesses. Requires 3 months of operational records. Simpler application than BOI. Good bridge financing if cash is tight at Month 4–6.

International grants

W
World Physiotherapy Development Fund
£2,000–£15,000 GBP
Funds physiotherapy practices in LMICs specifically. Goshen's evidence-based, outcomes-focused approach is exactly what they fund. Requires NMCN membership, clinical outcome data. Less competitive than other international grants. Apply Month 12.
U
USAID — Health Systems Strengthening Fund
$15,000–$75,000 USD
USAID Nigeria funds private sector health delivery that reduces burden on public health infrastructure. A clinic demonstrably reducing hospital wait times and improving community health outcomes is a strong candidate. Apply Month 18 with 18 months of outcome data.
G
Gates Foundation — Primary Healthcare Delivery
$25,000–$100,000 USD
Funds measurable health outcome improvement in LMICs. Goshen's year-on-year recovery time reduction (5–20%) is a compelling measurable narrative. Requires formal research proposal and institutional partner (approach UI or LAUTECH). Target Year 2–3 with 18+ months of outcome data.
